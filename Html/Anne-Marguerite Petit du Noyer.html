Anne-Marguerite Petit du Noyer

Document

Anne - Marguerite du Noyer ( Nîmes , 2 June 1663 — Voorburg , May 1719 ) was one of the most famous early 18th century female journalists . Her reports of the negotiations leading to the Peace of Utrecht were read all over Europe and admired for the distinction with which she reported on scandal and gossip . Born a Protestant she converted to Catholicism in the years of the Huguenot persecution . In 1686 she married Guillaume du Noyer . In 1701 she converted back to Calvinism and had to leave France in the event . She first moved to Geneva , then to The Hague . Among her famous visitors was Voltaire in 1713 .
