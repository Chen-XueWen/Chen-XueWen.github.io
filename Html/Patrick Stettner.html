Patrick Stettner is an American film director and writer . His first film , Flux , was released in 1996 as a short , and starred Allison Janney in an early role . He went on to direct and write the screenplay for The Business of Strangers in 2001 , which earned him a Grand Jury Prize nomination at the Sundance Film Festival of that year and earned lead actress Stockard Channing an AFI nomination for Best Female Actress . In 2006 , he then wrote and directed The Night Listener , an adaptation of Armistead Maupin 's eponymous semi - autobiographical 2000 novel , which landed less than stellar reviews from critics . He has not directed any films since The Night Listener . He is an alumnus of Columbia University School of the Arts ' Film division .
